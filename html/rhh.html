RHH Hope & Roots

Objective

Create an application that delivers referral information to GAR Clients as part of Mothers Matter Centre’s Reviving Hope & Home program.

Tools

React Native, Expo, Google Drive API, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe After Effects

Position

Co-op Position at Mothers Matter Centre

Partner Organizations

IRCC, ISSofBC, The Rumie Initiative

Role

  • Coded mobile application using React Native and Expo
  • Handled technological aspects of project
  • Assisted with graphic design

The Mothers Matter Centre (MMC) is a national, virtual consortium of organizations that empowers children and families by supporting and educating mothers with the HIPPY program (Home Instruction for Parents of Preschool Youngsters). This program employs mothers from socially-isolated and low income communities, training them as home visitors who come to the home of mothers in their community to deliver short activities to do with their preschool-aged child throughout the week. This prepares children for kindergarten and gets mothers involved with their child’s education.

As part of their research, MMC found that refugee mothers were more intensely isolated, and heavily relied on their Home Visitors, often requiring extra help to access resources and translate curriculum. Refugee mothers also had more urgent issues to handle than their child’s education, including housing, employment, and mental health challenges related to stress and trauma. This brought the development of Reviving Hope and Home (RHH), a program designed for Government Assisted Refugees using core HIPPY elements in order to make settlement a smoother process.

RHH was funded by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada and involves several partner organizations, including The Rumie Initiative. The Rumie Initiative is an organization that gives underserved communities access to education through Rumie Tablets and LearnCloud.

Mobile App Reasoning

I suggested that RHH have its own app, since a key issue before RHH was HIPPY Home Visitors and Coordinators were being asked by mothers for the same resources and information over and over again, each time going through a time-consuming chain of people before a referral is given to the mother. By centralizing this information in an app, RHH staff can easily reference the app when a mother needs information. Creating an app also separates the referrals from the learning and extension activities we would include on the LearnCloud.

Design and Programming Decisions

Since Rumie Tablets run on Android, the project was started using Android Studio. However, the committee thought it would ideal to make an app that would be available for both Android and iOS. Searching online brought me to React Native and Expo. React Native is a mobile derivative of React, a javascript library developed by Facebook for creating UI components for websites. React Native is currently being used for Facebook, Pinterest, Uber and more. This is paired with Expo, a framework for React Native, giving developers the ability to code for both Android and iOS with the same Javascript code. It also provides tools to get projects up and running quickly, such as navigators, UI elements, and more. This was key as I was new to Javascript and React Native at the start of this project.

The backend of the application uses Google Drive API, particularly for Google Sheets and Google Docs. Using these tools allows for RHH staff who are not skilled in computer programming or databases to be able to manipulate the app should anything need changing.

Since resources and information may expand or change over time, the application needed to be able to handle content dynamically. And with some essential needs requiring more than one resource, the app needed to support sheets linked to sheets, creating folders of resources. To do this, the app uses recursive functions to update and manage its data.

Each category of resources includes an icon to provide a visual representation, as well as a translation in Arabic and Farsi to help clients who may have difficulties with using English to navigate the app.

Challenges

Being new to mobile development, Javascript and React Native challenged my ability to learn quickly and troubleshoot code. Due to these tools being relatively new, finding solutions for errors was often difficult. Searching online was sometimes fruitless, leading myself to consult forums. Without any responses, I turned to attending React Developer Meetups to learn more from presentations and speak to peers for help. Additionally, I reached out to a trusted professor from SFU for support. Overall I was on the right track with finding solutions online, it was just a matter of finding the right search terms and the digging for the right webpages. Ultimately asking for help gave me a chance to reflect on my issue and I was able to solve them on my own.

Focus Group and Results

As part of the co-op position, one learning objective was to lead a focus group to get feedback from RHH Home Visitors about the app. While I had completed user research and testing in other projects, it was important for me to have experience in a professional environment. To ensure I was prepared for the focus group, I reviewed past assignments and readings from my courses. I also prepared an activity to help Home Visitors familiarize themselves with the app and practice using it. However, I was pleased to find Home Visitors coming prepared with questions and eager to provide feedback. I was able to run the focus group dynamically, with no need to use the activity, giving the group more time to give comments. With their suggestions for improvements I am now able to finalize the app as the work term comes to an end.
