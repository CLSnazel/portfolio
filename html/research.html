User Research + Design Intervention

Objective

Based on gathered user research from a partner organization, create a design intervention to improve the experience within the organization

Tools

Ethnography research, User workshop, Adobe Illustrator, Photography, Sketching, Prototyping

Class

Interaction Design Methods, Spring 2017, Team of 4

Team Members

Hayley Harkema, Erica Chu, Robin George

Role

  • Contributed to team’s design process by taking notes, sketching, prototyping and iterating ideas.
  • Created display cards for critique sessions
  • Created digital renders of designs in context

The Client

USRS is a non-profit that rescues invasive animals, often forfeited exotic pets. During our project, they were at capacity, caring for over 400 animals. They rely on dedicated volunteers to maintain the organization.

Our team researched the volunteering and the training process. We designed a system with volunteer name tags, a redesigned daily checklist, and instructions for volunteers to use as they work. I created digital renderings of the name tags and instructions to convey our concepts to teaching staff.

Volunteer Instructions

We targeted instructions because volunteers struggled with remembering instructions, they had to work independently, and staff were unavailable to answer questions. Signage for each animal provided information to visitors. We recreated the existing signs to expand, so volunteers can access instructions.

This initial design wasn’t easily manipulated or removable. To improve it, we made a case that holds portable task cards for volunteers, with the front listing the animal’s information. These were laminated to protect them in the animal care environment.

Name Tags

We designed the nametags as part of a volunteer skill advancement system in the renewed checklist. Volunteers didn’t always know their boundaries at USRS, and in past cases, volunteers overreached their abilities and put animals at risk. Name tags would also show who else they can ask for help. Each personal name tag had a star rating, with stickers added as the volunteer progressed.

However, each personal name tag would cost materials, and displaying a volunteer’s name could break their personal privacy. Star ratings also wasn’t true to USRS’s branding. Together we chose new volunteer skill titles like “Apprentice Aardvark”, and redesigned it to fit with the client's brand.

Final Showcase

For the showcase, I created display card that explained what each artifact was meant to do, the research that supports the need for it, and how the research influenced our design decisions.

We tied for first place at the showcase based on audience voting. We were unique for using a physical medium, we were very grounded in our research, and made a practical solution for the client.

Learning Reflection

While this class taught me a lot about user research, the hands on experience of working with a client showed me the amount of care and attention user research requires. Through our studies, we witnessed a power-dynamic issue where having staff and volunteers in the same room with us may have discouraged the volunteers from speaking up about their thoughts and ideas. It was also valuable for me to create designs that were directly related to the research we did, and be able provide evidence for our design reasoning.
